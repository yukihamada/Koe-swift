Frequently Asked Questions

How do I grant microphone access?

Go to System Settings > Privacy & Security > Microphone > Enable Koe.

How do I grant Accessibility access?

Koe uses Accessibility to type text into your active application. Go to System Settings > Privacy & Security > Accessibility > Enable Koe. This is required for the core voice input functionality.

Voice recognition is slow

Koe uses Whisper (local AI model) which requires initial model download (~500MB). On Apple Silicon Macs, recognition takes ~1.5 seconds. On Intel Macs, Apple's built-in speech recognition is used instead.

How to use meeting transcription?

Press Option+Command+M to start meeting mode. Koe will continuously record and transcribe your speech with timestamps. Press again to stop and generate an AI summary.

How to connect iPhone?

Both devices must be on the same Wi-Fi network. Open Koe on iPhone, tap "Connect to Mac", and enter the 4-digit PIN shown on your Mac's notification.

Privacy & Data

All voice processing happens locally on your device. No audio or text data is ever sent to external servers. Koe is open source — you can verify this yourself on GitHub.

System Requirements

  • Mac: macOS 13.0 or later (Apple Silicon recommended)
  • iPhone: iOS 17.0 or later
  • ~500MB disk space for the Whisper AI model

About Accessibility Usage

Koe uses the macOS Accessibility API to type recognized text directly into your active application. This is the core functionality of Koe — converting your voice into text input in any app. Without Accessibility access, Koe cannot insert text into other applications.

Koe also uses Accessibility for:

  • Typing recognized text into any active application
  • IME (Input Method Editor) switching between languages
  • Keyboard shortcut simulation for voice commands

Koe does NOT use Accessibility to monitor, track, or collect any user data.
